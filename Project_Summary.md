# Trader Performance vs Market Sentiment Analysis

## Methodology

This project investigates the relationship between Bitcoin market sentiment and trader performance using the Fear & Greed Index and Hyperliquid historical trading data.

The analysis began with data cleaning and preprocessing, including handling missing values, removing duplicates, and standardizing date formats. Both datasets were merged at the daily level using the trade date to assign a sentiment label (Extreme Fear, Fear, Neutral, Greed, or Extreme Greed) to each trade.

Several analytical metrics were created, including total profit and loss (PnL), win rate, average trade size, trade frequency, long/short trading behavior, and trader activity segments. Traders were also classified into Frequent and Infrequent groups based on trading activity to compare behavioral patterns and performance.

The merged dataset was analyzed using Tableau dashboards and visualizations to identify relationships between market sentiment and trader outcomes.

## Key Insights

### 1. Extreme Greed Periods Deliver the Highest Profitability

Average trader profitability was highest during Extreme Greed conditions. This suggests that traders perform better when market momentum is strong and directional trends are clearer.

### 2. Fear Periods Encourage Larger Position Sizes

Average trade size peaked during Fear periods, indicating that traders tend to increase exposure during market uncertainty. However, larger position sizes did not consistently translate into higher profitability.

### 3. Win Rates Improve During Bullish Sentiment

The highest win rates were observed during Extreme Greed periods, while Extreme Fear periods recorded the lowest success rates. This indicates that market sentiment has a measurable impact on trading outcomes.

### 4. Infrequent Traders Outperform Frequent Traders

Infrequent traders generated higher average profits compared to frequent traders. This finding suggests that trade quality and selectivity may be more important than trading volume.

## Strategy Recommendations

### Strategy 1: Increase Exposure During Extreme Greed

During Extreme Greed periods, traders may benefit from momentum-based strategies, trend-following setups, and larger profit targets due to stronger market direction and higher win rates.

### Strategy 2: Apply Stricter Risk Management During Fear

During Fear and Extreme Fear periods, traders should consider reducing position sizes, tightening stop-loss levels, and avoiding excessive exposure, as larger trades during these conditions do not consistently improve profitability.

### Strategy 3: Prioritize Trade Quality Over Trade Quantity

The superior performance of infrequent traders suggests that disciplined trade selection may be more effective than frequent participation. Traders should focus on high-conviction setups rather than increasing trading activity.
